A CLOSER LOOK

Let's take a closer look at the familiar sight of the American Flag as it stands today!

                                           

                   

There are alternating rows of seven red stripes and six white stripes.  The stripes represent the original 13 colonies.  George Washington determined that red color represented the blood connection to England, while the white in between each red stripe represented the separation from the mother country.  Additionally, today there is a blue field, or canton, of 50 stars, each representing the 50 states of the Union.  Upon closer scrutiny, there are 5 rows of 6 stars alternating with 4 rows of 5 stars, or as described by the Presidential decree 9 staggered rows and 11 staggered columns This pattern had to be established in 1959/1960 to accommodate the addition of Alaska and Hawaii to the Union.  Prior to that time there were six rows of eight stars each.

Mathematical Concepts exhibited:  The stripes exhibit the concept of collinearity. The stripes themselves are certainly  segments , some of which are  the same designated length. The 6 lower alternating red and white stripes are congruent in length and the top 7 stripes are congruent in length. Additionally the stripes clearly are parallel line segments.
     Stars in the same row exhibit  the geometric property of betweeness, as well as, collinearity.
      The right most edge of the blue field displaying the stars can be viewed as a  transversal to the adjacent parallel stripes, moreover, the right most edge is perpendicular to the bottom of the blue field.
