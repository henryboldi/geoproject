GLOSSARY

  • angle of depression  The angle between the downward line of sight and the horizontal.

  • angle of elevation The angle between the upward line of sight and the horizontal

  • arc A figure consisting of two points on a circle and all the points needed to connect them by a single path

  • area The number of square units of space within the boundary of a closed region.

  • circle  The set of all points in a plane that are a given distance from a given point in the plane.

  • congruent segments  Segments that have the same length.

  • congruent tirangles Triangles in which all pairs of corresponding parts (angles and sides) are congruent.

  • concave polygons A polygon in which at least one of the interior angles has a measure greater than 180.

  • coplanar  Lying in the same plane.

  • diagonal  A segment joining two nonconsecutive vertices.

  • extremes The first and fourth terms of a proportion.  In the proportion a:b=c:d, a and d are the extremes.

  • fly  Length of flag or union.

  • hoist  Width of flag or union.

  • means  The second and third terms of a proportion.  In the proportion a:b=c:d, b and c are the means.

  • parallel lines  Coplanar lines that do not intersect.

  • perimeter The sum of the lengths of a polygon.

  • perpedicular  Intersecting a right angles.

  • plane  A surface such that if any two points on the surface are connected by a line, all points on the line are also on the surface.

  • proportion  An equation stating that two or more ratios are equal.

  • quadrilateral  A four sided polygon.

  • radius  A segment joining the center of a circle to a point on the circle.  Also, the length of such a segment.

  • ratio  A quotient of two numbers.

  • rectangle  A parallelogram in which at least on angle is a right angle.

  • right angle  An angle whose measure is 90 degrees.

  • similar polygons Polygons in which ratios of the measures of corresponding sides are equal and corresponding angles are equal.

  • transversal  A line that intersects two coplanar lines in two distinct points.

  • triangle  A three sided polygon.
