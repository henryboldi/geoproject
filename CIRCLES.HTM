The Original Flag 

                               

The original flag supposedly designed by  Betsy Ross in 1775, featured the 13 stars each representing one of the original colonies in a circular design.  The stars form
the circumference of the circle emblazoned on the blue field.

 

Mathematical concepts: The stripes exhibit the concept of collinearity. The stripes themselves are certainly  segments , some of which are  the same designated length. The 6 lower alternating red and white stripes are congruent in length and the top 7 stripes are congruent in length. Additionally the stripes clearly are parallel line segments.
     Stars in the same row exhibit  the geometric property of betweeness, as well as, collinearity. 
      It should be noted that the stars representing the 13 original colonies were placed in a circle so no colony would be higher than the next.  This follows directly from the locus definition of circle as:  the set of all points in a plane that are a given distance from a given point in the plane.
     The right most edge of the blue field displaying the stars as the circumference of the circle can be viewed as a  transversal to the adjacent parallel stripes, moreover, the right most edge is perpendicular to the bottom of the blue field.
