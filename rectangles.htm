RECTANGLES

  •      Clearly the American Flag is a rectangle because it is a parallelogram with one right angle.  Moreover as a rectangle, it has two congruent diagonals.
  •      The perimeter of a rectangle is 2 times length + 2 times width.  The perimeter of any flag, therefore is 2 times fly + 2 times hoist.
  •       The area of a rectangle is, or course, length times width.  In terms of the flag the area of any one flag is fly times hoist.
  • Remember, from the PROPORTIONS Page, all flags have to have a hoist of 1 and a fly of 1.9.  So all flags, if correctly manufactured, will be similar.  Therefore, it is important for you, as a observant geometry student, to recognize that:
  1.  the ratio of the perimeters of all flags will be:  hoist of specific flag/1
  2.  the ratio of the areas of all flags will be :  (hoist of specific flag)^2/1
