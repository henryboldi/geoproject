Your Assignment

Inevitably as a math teacher, I hear the question, "But how will I ever use this information in the real world?"  The purpose of this web site was to illustrate that even as familiar a symbol as the American Flag exhibits geometric concepts.

You are almost 75% done with the study of Geometry this year.  Hopefully your perspective has changed with your new geometric knowledge.  I suspect you see the world through new eyes.  Hopefully you now see geometric relationships in common objects.  

Your assignment, therefore, is to select a common object, from your everyday environment, interpret it from a geometric point of view, and demonstrate how that object exemplifies geometric principles.

You will work with two other people from the class (see team assignments).  You can choose any object(s) you like, and you can present your findings to the class in any manner your team decides.  Examples would be: posters, websites, overhead, video, etc.  You will be marked as a team on Content, Organization, Creativity, Delivery or Presentation, and Geometric Concepts.  You will have 2-3 weeks to work on the project, with limited classroom time made available to you.  

So put on your geometry bifocals, roll up your sleeves and get started, and most important---ENJOY!
