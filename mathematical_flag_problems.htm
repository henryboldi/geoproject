MATHEMATICAL FLAG PROBLEMS

                       

  1. A flag has dimensions 74.1 inches by 39 inches. Each short stripe has length of 39.  What fractional part of the flag is red?
  2. According  to ratios and proportions set down by law, could a flag manufacturer create a flag 130 inches by 78 inches?
  3. While strolling one morning to get a little sun, Heloise noticed that a 28 foot
    telephone pole cast a 35 foot shadow.  Nearby was flagpole that cast a 25 foot shadow.  How tall was the flag pole?
  4. Find the height of a flag pole, if at point A, the angle of elevation of the top of the flag pole is 30 degrees, but one meter closer to the flag pole, at point C, the angle of elevation is 35 degrees.
  5.  When a five foot flagpole is mounted in a bracket on a wall, the top of the flag is 3 feet from the top of the wall.  What is the angle formed between the flagpole and the wall?
  6. If there is a 30 degree angle of elevation from a point 50 feet away from a flagpole, how high is the flagpole?
