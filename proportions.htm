Standard Proportions

Hoist (width) of flag (A) 1.0
Fly (length) of flag (B) 1.9
Hoist (width) of Union (C) 0.5385 (7/13)
Fly (length) of Union (D) 0.76
(E) 0.054
(F) 0.054
(G) 0.063
(H) 0.063
Diameter of star (K) 0.0616
Width of stripe (L) 0.0769 ( 1/13)

This table establishes standard proportions of the flag regardless of variations in overall size.  This means that all flags will be similar in shape, not necessarily congruent unless two flags are the same size.

Section 21 of the Executive Order No.10834, fixes only eleven sizes of flags for government use.  The largest of these is 20 feet in hoist and thirty eight feet in fly.  The smallest one is one and thirty-two hundredths feet in hoist and two and one half feet in fly.

MATHEMATICAL PROBLEM: Use the above ratios in a proportion.  Does the product of means approximate the product of the extremes? (Remember we're working with actual cloth here!)
